The Journey Begins: Troposphere

The troposphere is the lowest layer of Earth's atmosphere, extending from the surface up to about 8-15 kilometers high. This is where weather happens and where we live.

Into the Blue: Stratosphere

The stratosphere extends from about 15km to 50km above Earth. This layer contains the ozone layer, which protects us from harmful UV radiation.

Shooting for the Stars: Mesosphere

The mesosphere extends from about 50km to 85km above Earth. It's the coldest layer of the atmosphere, with temperatures dropping to -90°C. Meteors burn up in this layer.

The Final Frontier: Thermosphere

The thermosphere extends from about 85km to 600km above Earth. Despite high temperatures, it would feel cold to us because the gas molecules are far apart. The International Space Station orbits here.

The Edge of Space: Exosphere

The exosphere is the outermost layer of Earth's atmosphere, extending from about 600km to 10,000km above Earth. This is where atoms and molecules escape into space.
